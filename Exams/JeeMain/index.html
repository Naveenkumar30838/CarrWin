    About Jee Main

    The Joint Entrance Examination (Main), commonly known as JEE Main, is an all-India standardized computer-based test for admission to various undergraduate engineering programs(B.E / B.tech) in India. It is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and other Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs). The exam is also an eligibility test for JEE Advanced, which is conducted for admission to the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs).

    Eligibility

    1. Nationality :

    You must be an Indian citizen, but if you're a foreign national, NRI, PIO, or OCI, you can still apply.

    2. Age Requirements :

    There's no age limit, and if you completed your 12th grade in 2023, 2022, or you're doing it in 2024, you're eligible.

    3. Total Attempts

    You can try the exam three times in a row. The test happens twice a year, so that's up to six attempts.

    4. Qualifying Exam

    Your qualifying exams could be your 12th-grade final exams from recognized boards, like CBSE. You can also qualify through a two-year Pre-University or Intermediate exam from a recognized university or board. Or, if you've finished the National Defense Academy's Joint Services Wing course. There are other ways too, but you need to have studied specific subjects, including Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry, a Language, and one more subject.

    5. Year Of Passing Qualifying Exam

    You should've passed your 12th-grade or equivalent exam in 2023 or 2022, or you're going to in 2024.

    6. Diploma Holders

    If you have a diploma, you can apply, but your rank and score won't count for NITs or CFTIs.

    7. Marks Eligibility

    For B.Tech, if you're in the General category, you need at least 75%, or if you're in the reserved category, at least 65%, or be in the top 20 percentile. For B.Arch, you should score 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics in your 12th-grade or equivalent.

    8. JEE Main 2023 Eligibility for Repeaters

    There's no age limit. You must've passed the 12th class exam in 2021 or 2022. Score at least 75% in the qualifying exam, with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects. Only the top 2,50,000 Main exam qualifiers can take the Advanced exam.

    State Code of Eligibility

    This is based on where you passed your Class XII exam. It's not about where you were born or where you live. For example, if you took your 12th-grade exam in Delhi, your state code is Delhi, even if you're originally from somewhere else. If you're studying through NIOS, choose the state where your study center is. If you're an Indian citizen in Nepal or Bhutan, it's based on your passport's permanent address.

    Once you meet these criteria and pass all the stages of the JEE 2023 exam, you can enroll in B.Tech, B.E, B.Arch, and Integrated MSc programs at prestigious engineering schools like NITs and IIITs.

    Steps After Jee

    After the JEE Main exam, here are the subsequent steps and timelines for the JEE Advanced exam and counselling:

    1. JEE Main Result:

    Jee Mains Result are usually announced within a few weeks after the exam, typically around one month after the exam date.

    2. JEE Advanced:

    Examination Timing: JEE Advanced is usually conducted about one to two months after the declaration of JEE Main results.

    Eligibility: Top candidates (Usually 2,50,000) based on JEE Main ranks are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.

    3. Counselling and Seat Allotment:

    Authority : JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) conducts counselling for admission to IIT's , NIT's, IIIT's, GFTI's, and other participating institutes based on JEE Main ranks.

    Timeline : JoSAA counselling begins shortly after the declaration of JEE Advanced results, typically around one to two weeks after the exam.

    Choice Filling : Candidates fill their choices of institutes and courses based on their JEE Advanced rank for IIT and Jee Main Ranks for NIT and other .

    Seat Allotment : JoSAA conducts multiple rounds of seat allotment based on preferences, merit, and seat availability.

    Flexibility in Choices : Candidates can modify and re-prioritize their choices during each round of counselling.

    4. Document Verification and Admission:

    After seat allotment, candidates need to report to the allocated institute for document verification.

    Pay the required fees and complete other formalities to secure admission.

    Important Dates

    Registration Period : December/January

    Exam Dates : February/March/April

    Admit Card Download : Few weeks before the exam

    Result Declaration : April/May

    Registration and Choice Filling : May/June

    Seat Allotment : June/July

    Document Verification : July

    Admission Offers : Finalized by July/August

    Admission Deadline : Completed by August/September

    WHY ?

    1. Gateway to prestigious engineering institutes:
    JEE Main is the primary entrance exam for admission into various prestigious engineering colleges in India, including the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and other centrally funded technical institutes (CFTIs). These institutes are renowned for their quality education, research facilities, and industry connections, making them highly sought-after by students.

    2. Primary Exam for Jee Advanced
    JEE Main also serves as a preparatory step for the JEE Advanced exam, which is the entrance exam for the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). A good rank in JEE Main can give students a chance to appear for JEE Advanced, which is considered the most prestigious engineering entrance exam in India.

    3. Wide range of engineering courses:
    JEE Main opens doors to a wide range of undergraduate engineering courses across various specializations. Students can choose from disciplines like computer science, mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, civil engineering, and many more based on their interests and aspirations.

    4. Exposure to a competitive environment:
    Preparing for and taking JEE Main helps students test their knowledge, identify their strengths and weaknesses, and develop a competitive spirit. The experience prepares them for the rigors of demanding engineering curriculums and future professional challenges.

    5. Standardized benchmark for engineering aptitude :
    JEE Main provides a standardized platform to assess a student's aptitude and knowledge in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, which are core subjects in most engineering disciplines. The rigorous exam helps identify talented individuals with the potential to excel in demanding engineering programs.

    6. Sense of achievement :
    Clearing JEE Main is a significant achievement that instills confidence and a sense of self-belief in students. It validates their hard work, dedication, and talent, motivating them to pursue their engineering dreams with greater motivation and resolve.

    Overall, taking the JEE Main exam is a wise decision for students who are serious about pursuing a career in engineering. It provides them with an opportunity to test their skills, gain valuable exposure to the competitive landscape, and potentially secure admission to some of the best engineering institutes in India.
